Process

1 |

Research

First things first. It's time to identify business and user problems and needs. Depending on the circumstances and the unknowns, I'll use an sortment of research methods like interviews, focus groups, observations, surveys, five why's, rose-thorn-bud, card-sorting-like activities, importance difficulty matrixes, and swim lanes.

2 |

Wireframing

Now it's time to hash out some ideas. Normally, to start I like to just stick to pen and papper to push out ideas. Sometimes, this can be done with your team or even a group of users to see how others are thinking about and breed new ideas. After pushing out a handful of ideas, I then choose some to quickly make wireframes on the computer.

3 |

Usability Testing

Depending on time contrants and participant availability I'll choose between a handful of prototyping methods to get some quick feedback. Methods used in the past include paper prototyping, HTML/CSS prototypes, and wireframing tools (figma, sketch with invision, etc).

Note: Will make the decision to either go back to the drawing board to understand and try another solution or iterate on the tested solution(s).

4 |

UI Design

This is focusing on finalizing the layout, adding color, and other pixel perfecting to make it on brand.

5 |

Document for Development

Given the workflow and designs have been solidified, then it's time to complete documentation of stories and acceptance criteria for developers to easily understand.
